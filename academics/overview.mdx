The `/gestion-academica` page is the central hub for building your school's academic skeleton. Every other academic operation — attendance, grades, teacher assignments — depends on the structure you define here. Before enrolling students or recording grades, you must set up the hierarchy of grade years, sections, subjects, and study plans.

## Core entities

The academic structure is built from four interconnected entities that form a strict hierarchy:

- **Grade years (`anios`)** — the top-level grouping, representing each year of schooling (e.g., "1er Año", "2do Año"). Each year has a unique numeric level.
- **Sections (`secciones`)** — classroom groups within a grade year for a given school period (e.g., Section A of 1er Año, 2024–2025). A section belongs to exactly one grade year.
- **Subjects (`materias`)** — the subject catalog entries (e.g., "Matemática", "Lengua"). Subjects exist independently and are linked to grade years through study plans.
- **Study plans (`planes_de_estudio`)** — the join between a subject and a grade year, defining which subjects are taught in each year. Created automatically when you add a subject.
- **Teacher assignments (`seccion_docente_materia`)** — links a specific teacher (`docente`) to a subject within a specific section, forming the teaching load.

## Relationship flow

First create grade years, then create sections within each year, then add subjects and link them to years via the study plan, then assign teachers to subjects within specific sections.
